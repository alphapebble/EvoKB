### Introduction

Activity-Stream Engineering is a technique for capturing the pulse of AI-agents by replicating human narrative memory and enabling agents to see the "movie" of work rather than just a snapshot of state.

### What is Activity-Stream Engineering?

Activity-Stream Engineering is an approach that uses activity streams, built on principles of event sourcing, stateful stream processing, and graph topology, to capture semantic intent and reasoning in AI-agents. This allows agents to see the "movie" of work and enable them to learn or audit processes.

### Key Claims and Evidence

* Activity-Stream Engineering replicates human narrative memory.
* Agents can see the "movie" of work rather than just a snapshot of state.
* The foundation of Process RAG is the activity stream, built on principles of event sourcing, stateful stream processing, and graph topology.

### Architecture: Coordinated Progress as a Graph

The architecture of Activity-Stream Engineering views computation as a graph. Nodes represent microservices, FaaS functions, and AI engineering systems, while edges represent communication mediums (RPC, Kafka Streams, Event Buses). Workflows are connected sub-graphs that represent logical pieces of business value.

### The High-Velocity Reasoning Fabric

The high-velocity reasoning fabric is a layer where agents don't just act but react to the shifting topology of the business. This allows for complete replayability and enables time-travel debugging, parallel reality simulation, and in-flight context injection.

### Advanced: Inference via Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) can reason over topology and are used for deep process inference. GNNs learn from graph topology and can convert complex multi-agent process sub-graphs into vector embeddings.

### Stream Processing Patterns

Stream processing patterns include:

* Windowing for task inference
* Stateful traces

These patterns enable the creation of a "process reality" by applying patterns from Apache Flink.

### Conclusion

Activity-Stream Engineering is an approach that captures the pulse of AI-agents by replicating human narrative memory and enabling agents to see the "movie" of work. The high-velocity reasoning fabric enables complete replayability, while GNNs provide a means for deep process inference.
