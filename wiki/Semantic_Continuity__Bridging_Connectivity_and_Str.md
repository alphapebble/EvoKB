#### Introduction

Semantic Continuity is a framework for building a Universal Ontology that bridges connectivity and structured knowledge. It provides a solution to the problem of siloed realities in enterprise systems, enabling cross-domain reasoning, zero-shot accuracy, and governance at scale.

#### Key Claims and Evidence

* **The Problem**: Siloed realities in enterprise systems lead to divergent definitions and conflicting data.
* **The Solution**: Grounding technical architecture in two specific enterprise assets: Operational Domain Model and Enterprise Taxonomy.
* **Key Insight**: A universal enterprise ontology emerges not by design alone, but by grounding semantics in business reality.

#### Theoretical Foundation

* Promise Theory provides a formal framework for how independent agents can collaborate through voluntary "promises" rather than imposed commands.
* RDF, OWL, and SHACL are established standards for representing, reasoning, and validating enterprise data.

#### The Three Pillars of Continuity

1. **Structure (System of Record)**: Raw data in its source system.
2. **Meaning (Enterprise Taxonomy)**: Human-level definition of business reality.
3. **Connection (Universal Ontology)**: AI-native layer that maps raw structure to human meaning.

#### Strategic Advantage

* Companies that achieve Semantic Continuity gain:
	+ Cross-Domain Reasoning
	+ Zero-Shot Accuracy
	+ Governance at Scale
