**Precedent Engineering: Teaching Agents the 'Why' Behind Decisions**
===========================================================

**Tags:** AI Engineering Systems, Decision Systems, Organizational Learning

**Summary:** Precedent engineering is a method for capturing decision traces and human judgment to enable AI agents to learn from organizational precedent and improve over time. This playbook provides guidance on how to implement precedent engineering in AI systems.

**Front Matter**

Precedent engineering is a crucial aspect of building autonomous AI systems that can learn from organizational wisdom. By capturing decision traces and human judgment, agents can develop the skills to make decisions on their own without relying solely on algorithms.

**Phase 0: The Dark Context Problem**
-------------------------------------

### The Problem

Systems of record capture what happened, but they don't capture why. This is known as dark context, which is critical to decisions but invisible to systems.

### Solution

Decision traces are immutable records that capture not just the outcome, but the reasoning behind it. By capturing decision traces, agents can develop a deeper understanding of organizational judgment and make more informed decisions.

**Phase 1: Decision Trace Anatomy**
-----------------------------------

### The Problem

Not all decisions are equal. Capturing everything creates noise, while capturing too little misses the judgment that matters.

### Solution

The exception-first schema focuses on exceptions and overrides—the moments where humans deviated from the default. These are the highest-signal decisions that can be used to capture organizational judgment.

**Phase 2: The Precedent Graph**
-------------------------------

### The Problem

Individual decision traces are useful, but the real power emerges when they connect—forming a precedent graph of organizational judgment.

### Solution

Similarity-based retrieval is used to query the precedent graph for similar past situations. This allows agents to make decisions based on precedents rather than just algorithms.

**Phase 3: Earning Agent Autonomy**
----------------------------------

### The Problem

Agents today operate in two modes: fully autonomous or always-human-in-the-loop. Neither scales.

### Solution

Agents earn autonomy by demonstrating they can match human judgment on precedented cases. This is achieved through a hierarchy of agent behaviors, with increasing levels of autonomy based on precedent similarity and outcome success rate.
